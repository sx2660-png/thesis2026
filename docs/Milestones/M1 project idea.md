学生 + “客户”（例如实习单位或其他组织，他们将提供项目的目标和限制，或者可以实施你的最终设计）。

When I received my internship offer from a company in the electricity trading and virtual power plant domain, I immediately realized that it could become a meaningful industry-collaborative project for this course. I proposed the idea of transforming my internship work into a company-partnered research and development project, and luckily, the company strongly supported this direction.

In real company workflows, traders and operators still spend a significant amount of time manually interpreting policy documents, checking declaration rules, and reviewing operational risks before execution. This inspired my central research question: can a multi-agent system transform electricity market rules into machine-readable knowledge and provide risk-aware decision support?

Initially, I considered focusing on load forecasting as an independent module. However, after discussions with Dr. Xu and further investigation into the field, I realized that load forecasting is already a highly crowded research area with a high technical barrier and relatively slow short-term impact. As a result, I decided to shift the project focus toward compliance-aware multi-agent orchestration, especially in the areas of policy understanding, risk control, and workflow coordination.

Currently, I have been conducting preliminary research in two main directions:

- LangGraph-based multi-agent system design
    
- Risk control mechanisms in electricity trading, including deviation risk, real-time price risk, and compliance constraints
    

Building on this foundation, I hope to collaborate with the company to develop a LangGraph-based multi-agent prototype for electricity trading operations and compliance review.

I understand that this is a relatively large engineering project. The company hopes to complete the initial development during the summer and aims to begin practical deployment around September. Fortunately, this timeline aligns closely with the schedule and milestones of this course, making it a valuable opportunity to connect academic research with real-world industry implementation.

I recognize that right now this is quite a large undertaking. I hope that in the next few weeks, I will be able to continue refining my idea and narrow it to a scope that is manageable to actually execute, research, and analyze in two semesters.
