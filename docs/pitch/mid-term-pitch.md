# Mid-term Pitch

The mid-term pitch was designed for a general audience unfamiliar with electricity trading. It opened with familiar household electricity use and then translated a large market quantity into an everyday scale. The purpose was to make the consequences of trading decisions understandable before introducing the technical system.

The presentation then introduced the day-ahead decision problem: a trader must decide whether an automated strategy can continue, should be recalculated, or should pause for human review. Trading Brain was presented as a multi-agent decision-support system that combines news, policy rules, market signals, and human judgment.

## What the pitch needed to communicate

- Why electricity trading decisions matter beyond the trading desk.
- Why information from news, rules, forecasts, and market data is difficult to interpret quickly.
- How the system could explain a warning rather than only produce a score.
- Why the trader remains responsible for the final action.

## Feedback that shaped the next stage

Feedback on the pitch asked for more concrete explanations of consequences, clearer trigger mechanisms behind recommendations, and a walkthrough showing the prototype in use. These points helped move the project toward evidence transparency, human review, and a more explicit interaction design.
