# Feedback

## From early project review

Early feedback supported the value of the industry partnership but also pointed out that the original project scope was too broad. This helped shift the focus away from building an independent forecasting system and toward policy interpretation, risk control, and a manageable decision-support prototype.

## From the mid-term pitch

The pitch feedback encouraged clearer everyday explanations of the consequences of electricity trading decisions. It also asked for more transparency about what triggers a recommendation and suggested showing the prototype in use. These comments became design requirements for evidence display, trigger explanations, and the human review path.

## From June 27 project feedback

The project should be understood as a human-computer interaction and decision-support study, not only as an agent workflow. The feedback encouraged rapid prototyping, staged testing, and clearer questions about whether the system helps people notice anomalies, interpret evidence, calibrate trust, and choose an intervention.

## How feedback changed the project

Across these stages, the project moved from “Can I build a multi-agent electricity trading system?” toward “How can an AI system support a trader's judgment without hiding uncertainty or taking away responsibility?” This change affects the prototype, the evaluation criteria, and the way the final thesis will discuss success.
