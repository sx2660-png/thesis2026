# Updates

## June 17, 2026

The first stage focused on improving evidence and alert levels. The project separated live event evidence from candidate evidence, background references, and search failures. The goal was to prevent old policy documents, undated materials, or unrelated regional news from producing an exaggerated warning.

The prototype also clarified the relationship between the online warning layer and offline market-data replay. The May 25-27 case was used to test whether the system could identify the known abnormal window while treating later data as validation rather than as information available before the original decision.

## June 29, 2026

The alert logic was replayed across May 15-31 and the full month of May. The current replay identified May 25, May 26, and May 27 as the initial review window, while treating May 28 as continuation and May 29 as a day that no longer met the full abnormal pattern.

This iteration moved the project from asking only whether the system could raise an alert to asking whether the alert could be explained. The next step is to confirm the enterprise interfaces and continue improving the quality of time-stamped, Guangdong-specific evidence.

## Current status

The prototype workflow and evidence logic are in progress. The main remaining work is human-centred testing, interface refinement, and clarification of how the warning layer could connect to enterprise prediction, strategy, and execution systems.
